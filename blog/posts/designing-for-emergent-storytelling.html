Essay · February 2024

Designing for Emergent Storytelling

Interaction design can borrow from game systems and speculative fiction to build narratives that flex with audience input without losing coherence.

Story as system

Rather than writing linear scripts, I frame experiences as systems. Characters become states, scenes are nodes, and transitions are governed by rules that balance tension, curiosity, and user intent.

Signals that guide the arc

Adaptive stories hinge on meaningful signals. I track context (who the audience is), cadence (how often they interact), and resonance (what keeps them engaged). These signals inform which narrative arc the system surfaces next.

Building with modular media

Generating assets on the fly doesn’t have to feel disjointed. By designing modular narrative blocks—audio cues, visual motifs, dialog fragments—I can reorder pieces while maintaining tone and theme.
